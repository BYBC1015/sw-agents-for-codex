# Local Tourism Film Proposal Reference

Use this reference for local culture-tourism bureaus, city image films, destination promotional films, regional brand videos, tourism investment decks, government-facing video production proposals, or similar public-sector tourism communication work.

## What The Client Usually Cares About

Local tourism and government-facing clients often care about:

- city image and destination recognition
- seasonal tourism window
- tourist motivation and conversion
- public-sector reporting confidence
- media or TV broadcast quality
- multi-scene use: leader report, tourism promotion, investment event, social media, press, exhibition
- whether the film expresses local value rather than only showing pretty scenery

## Market Trend To Film Task

Use minimal research to decide the film task.

If tourism data is growing:

- Do not frame the film as rescuing the market.
- Treat the film as brand upgrade, destination memory, and communication asset building.
- Move from "seeing scenery" to "remembering the city and wanting to depart".

If tourism data is flat or declining:

- Avoid harsh language such as "market failure".
- Use terms such as recognition repair, visit reason rebuilding, confidence restoration, or destination value clarification.

## Recommended Slide Flow

For a 15-25 page city image film proposal, start from this flow:

```text
1. Cover
2. Agenda
3. Market / context judgment
4. Communication task derived from the judgment
5. Film positioning
6. Creative proposition
7. Visual system
8. Narrative structure
9. Runtime / rhythm structure
10-15. Key content chapters
16. Camera / image method
17. Packaging / subtitles / motion language
18. Music / sound layer
19. Broadcast or delivery standard
20. Deliverables and next step
```

Do not force this flow when the client request is smaller. Keep the smallest useful version.

## Tourism Film Logic

A city tourism film should usually answer:

- Why should this place be remembered now?
- What visitor desire does it activate?
- What is the city value beyond scenic resources?
- How does the film serve the current season or campaign?
- How can the main film become reusable visual assets?

## Six-Part Narrative Option

For local tourism films, a six-part structure often works:

```text
Opening scale -> ecological/resource base -> seasonal experience -> people/culture -> industry/vitality -> invitation/brand close
```

Adapt the labels to the city. Do not write it as a rigid sightseeing list.

## Image Method

Keep the method credible but not too granular:

- aerial: scale and geography
- ground-level: immersion
- people: emotion and real life
- night: texture and atmosphere
- packaging: restrained maps, subtitles, motifs
- sound: music, narration, ambience, local texture

Do not jump into full storyboard unless the user asks for storyboard work.

## Visual Direction

For cinematic local tourism proposal decks:

- dark tone is not black; it should still show image detail
- use local color cues: land, grass, water, sky, warm light, seasonal accents
- use local or ethnic motifs lightly, not as decoration overload
- alternate strong image pages, text-led logic pages, and method/diagram pages
- avoid generic tech-business templates unless the client context requires them

## Delivery Standard

For government or broadcast-quality film proposals, delivery pages can mention:

- 4K or higher acquisition recommendation
- HD / 4K versions
- broadcast-safe audio mix when relevant
- unified color system
- main film, teaser, cutdowns, clean version, subtitle version, project archive

Keep standards truthful and aligned with actual production capacity.
